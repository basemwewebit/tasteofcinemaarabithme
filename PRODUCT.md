# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Modern Arab cinephiles and creative industry professionals who treat cinema as craft, not just entertainment. They value the artistry of a frame as much as the plot of a script.

Primary context is a lean-back, winding-down experience on mobile or tablet. They arrive seeking inspiration or a perspective-shifting long-form piece, not a quick news snippet. The job is 70% visual discovery and 30% deep reading.

## Product Purpose

A tastemaker platform that presents cinema culture as a curated digital gallery. The interface is the lighting and the frame: present but unobtrusive, guiding the reader through a journey of discovery rather than dumping them into a database.

Success looks like a reader losing track of time while browsing, then settling into a long article with the same sense of occasion as opening a quality print magazine.

## Brand Personality

Sophisticated, Dramatic, Authoritative.

Quiet luxury: confident enough not to scream for attention. Dramatic chiaroscuro: the play of light and shadow is editorial grammar, not decoration. Authoritative voice: the perspective of an expert, not a hobbyist.

## Anti-references

- Generic news portals: cluttered sidebars, red breaking-news tickers, clickbait density (e.g., Variety, Deadline layouts).
- The "Letterboxd" utility feel: heavy grids of raw poster data, star ratings, database aesthetics.
- Neon/gamer vibes: high-saturation neons, fast animations, aggressive motion. Think opera house, not gaming lounge.

## Design Principles

1. **Design as curation.** The UI is lighting and framing. It guides the eye without dominating the content.
2. **Lean-back immersion.** Prioritize calm, uninterrupted reading and browsing over urgency or density.
3. **Chiaroscuro confidence.** Embrace high contrast and shadow as editorial grammar, not decoration.
4. **Tastemaker authority.** Every surface should feel like an expert's recommendation, not an algorithmic feed.
5. **Discovery architecture.** The interface choreographs a journey; it does not present a raw catalog.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG 2.1 AA target for all new work.
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`: cinematic motion should never override user system preferences.
- Arabic RTL native: all layouts, focus states, and reading order must serve right-to-left Arabic text first.
- Maintain readable contrast ratios even in the dramatic dark palette; the chiaroscuro must not sacrifice legibility.
