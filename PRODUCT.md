# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Arabic-speaking film readers who come to Mazaq Cinema to discover reviews, ranked lists, analysis, and curated recommendations. They are browsing in a leisure and editorial context: deciding what to watch, following cinema conversations, or reading deeper criticism in Arabic.

## Product Purpose

Mazaq Cinema is an Arabic cinema magazine that turns film discovery into a curated editorial experience. Success means readers immediately feel they are inside a credible cinema publication, can find worthwhile articles quickly, and trust the site enough to keep reading, sharing, and returning.

## Brand Personality

Cinematic, editorial, trustworthy. The brand should feel atmospheric and film-literate without becoming obscure; confident enough to guide taste, but accessible enough for readers who want recommendations, lists, and analysis in clear Arabic.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic WordPress news and blog templates, Netflix-copycat red and black cliches, cold SaaS styling, weak Arabic typography, cluttered entertainment portals, clickbait visual systems, and any interface that treats film imagery as decoration instead of editorial substance.

## Design Principles

1. Cinema leads the surface: posters, stills, editorial hierarchy, and pacing should make the site feel film-native before a reader reads a word.
2. Arabic typography is part of the product: preserve connected letterforms, strong RTL rhythm, generous reading measures, and a clear split between display, body, and numeric text.
3. Curation beats volume: emphasize featured articles, editor picks, most-read content, and category paths instead of flattening everything into equal cards.
4. Trust through restraint: use atmospheric drama, but keep navigation, metadata, ads, and calls to action calm, legible, and honest.
5. Performance protects immersion: loaders, motion, imagery, and ads must support reading and discovery rather than delaying or distracting from them.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Prioritize robust RTL support, readable Arabic typography, keyboard-visible focus states, reduced-motion behavior, color contrast checks across light and dark themes, touch targets that work on mobile, and semantic structure for screen readers.
